Step 2: don’t fixate on the per-edition unit price ← what really eats budget is the billing method + hidden costs + out-of-package add-ons

Core insight: software procurement is only about 20% of total cost of ownership (TCO). The per-seat price is just the entrance; the truly uncontrollable bulk sits in: ① the billing method (per-seat / per-conversation / per-module / hybrid · pick the wrong tier and idle seats or overage both burn money); ② hidden costs (knowledge-base training ~20k/quarter, data migration 30k–170k, annual maintenance = 15–25% of the initial install); ③ out-of-package add-ons (tickets / bots / marketing bots / phone callback are all stacked extras); ④ overage fees (the Enterprise edition includes 100,000 bot messages · beyond that you pay extra). Real-scenario quick reads

  • Micro / startup, just a few agents: start on the free trial edition (but the official limits are real — only 1 agent, 2 concurrent visitors, chat logs kept 30 days, no queue · anything slightly serious forces an upgrade); for a real price don’t trust the rumored 1888, use your actual seat count and ask the official site for a quote (note the ¥3500 paid entry threshold · a small order may not transact at the unit price).
  • Small/medium business, needs tickets + bots: remember that tickets/bots are out-of-package add-ons (historical prices: tickets 1888, bots 3888/16888); fold these stacked extras + the Enterprise edition’s 100,000-message overage fee into the annual budget, don’t look at the seat unit price alone.
  • Want to negotiate, tight budget: end of quarter / year-end, when vendors chase targets is the best window; tactics = requirement bundling (tickets + multilingual packaged) + cracking the tiered quote + promising referrals; the vendor blog’s self-reported case claims a three-year contract can shave 30%+ (mark it ‘per vendor case · not externally auditable’).
  • Torn between Meiqia vs Qiyu/Sobot/Holly: all four domestic vendors publish no figures officially — you can only ask for a quote; the rumored Qiyu 1888/3888 is also a 2022 restatement. You can only compare ‘openness’ (Udesk/Zendesk publish, the rest quote) and ‘who bundles tickets in the standard edition’ (Meiqia relatively does, Qiyu/Sobot need a separate purchase).
  • Torn between Meiqia vs Zendesk (going global): Zendesk officially publishes USD prices — Suite Professional from $115/seat/month, all add-ons on $265/month, 50 seats ~$159k/year (millions of RMB scale); Meiqia Flagship is on the order of 5888 yuan/seat/year — once overseas stacks add-ons it’s an order of magnitude above domestic, but the real pain point is Chinese localization / WeChat-Douyin integration, not price alone.
  • Worried about being trapped by hidden costs: audit by the three TCO ledgers — the AI-replacement ledger (look at intent-recognition / self-resolution rate, not ‘has an AI feature’), the hidden-ops ledger (knowledge-base training 20k/quarter · data migration 30k–170k · annual maintenance 15–25%), the opportunity-cost ledger (verify ISO27001 / MLPS Level 3); require milestone payments ±5% in the contract + a usage circuit breaker (auto-switch when over 150% of baseline) + a 3000-concurrency stress test.
